Motivation

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

Completely opposite of what it has been till date. I mean I have always believed that happiness should be the sole motive of life but the sustainability factor never occurred to me before. My aim now will be to find a purpose and work towards it instead of doing things for pleasure.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise,

One of my core values is helping people in need. I have always tried to help my family, friends or whoever I could whenever I could. I believe that when people are struggling and are in need, a helping hand can provide a huge support. Just knowing that you are not alone in the moment of adversity can give one a huge boost to keep fighting and can act as a great motivation.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

The ideas were very effective. Having a calm body and mind (inner calm) , I found that I was a lot more emotionally stable during the exercise than the actual experience. I could convince myself easily to let go of grasping and aversion and that the experiences were only physiological sensations, something temporary (emotional resilience) and that whatever the experience there's always light at the end of the tunnel (cognitive resilience).
