Daily Mindfulnees

What did you learn from your minfulness conversation?

I performed this exercise with my beloved wife. She usually has my full attention when we talk but this experience felt different. Knowing that she was listening and focusing sincerely to every word that I spoke, I was able to express myself much clearly. Not getting interrupted in the middle also helped me keep the flow. When it was her turn to talk, I found that as I listened to her there were thoughts arising inside of my head but when I tried to bring back the focus to her voice they slowly disappeared. So the most important learning for me was, It is ok for thoughts to arise inside my head. I need not try to force them away. Instead slowly shift my focus to the thing that needs attention.

How did the other person find it?

My wife loved the exercise “apart from the point where I was the one who was getting to do all the talking” (YOU CAN DELETE THE WORDS IN QUOTES SHAYAN ;) ). She really appreciated my efforts when I tried to explain to her what I thought she talked about, so that I could understand her correctly.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

I think I will start with an easy technique i.e. One mindful breath a day and slowly start increasing the number of mindful breaths per day and then occasionally try the other techniques.
